Let's talk about the Emotional Navigator.

You open it. You're asked: how are you feeling?

Not a clinical assessment. Not a mood meter with a smiley face. Just ten words. Calm. Anxious. Frustrated. Excited. Sad. Angry. Hopeful. Numb. Overwhelmed. Content.

You pick one. You rate the intensity. You note what triggered it, if you know. You note what helped.

That's it.

But here's what happens over time.

The system starts to see patterns. You always feel scattered on Monday mornings. You're most regulated after you walk. Overwhelm follows interrupted sleep.

These aren't insights a therapist gives you. These are patterns your own data reveals.

You learn to see yourself more clearly. Not to fix yourself. To understand yourself.

That's the point.
