Your senses are not just inputs. They're regulators.

The Sensory Check-In asks you to rate five channels. Visual. Auditory. Tactile. Body position. Internal signals.

Not to judge. Not to fix. Just to notice.

Because here's what most people learn too late: overwhelm isn't about what's happening. It's about what your nervous system is processing.

After a few weeks, you'll see it clearly. Bad sensory days precede bad mood days. Environments that drain you aren't random — they're patterns.

You stop fighting yourself. You start adjusting the environment instead.

That's the shift.
