Sensory overload isn't dramatic. It's quiet.

A headache you can't explain. Irritability that seems to come from nowhere. The sudden inability to tolerate sounds that were fine an hour ago.

Most people push through it. They add it to the list of things wrong with them.

The Sensory Check-In helps you see it coming. Because overload doesn't come from nowhere. It accumulates.

Fluorescent lights plus background chatter plus tight clothing plus low sleep plus one more notification.

The system doesn't judge. It just shows you the accumulation.

And once you can see it, you can adjust. Before the crash, not after.

That's the difference.
